About Me

I am currently a Network Administration student at New Brunswick Community College, bringing over three decades of practical technology experience to my formal transition toward IT infrastructure, networking, and cybersecurity.

As the founder of Hands Down Designs, I provide hands-on IT support, network configuration, and malware remediation services for small businesses, nonprofit organizations, and residential clients, supporting over twenty recurring clients and numerous one-time projects. Through this work, I regularly troubleshoot hardware and software issues, configure segmented networks, implement backup solutions, and collaborate with vendors to maintain reliable and secure systems. My interest in network administration developed naturally as I supported increasingly complex environments, including full network rebuilds, firewall configurations, and wireless infrastructure upgrades. In parallel, assisting clients affected by cyber incidents (including malware infections, compromised accounts, and system recoveries) highlighted the importance of proactive security measures, system hardening, and user education.

I am actively developing my technical expertise through my studies at NBCC and industry certifications, including CompTIA IT Fundamentals (ITF+) and CompTIA A+ (Core 1). I believe that strong technical fundamentals, continuous learning, and real-world problem-solving are essential to becoming an effective and adaptable IT professional.

Industry Research

IT Employment Trends in Canada and Atlantic Canada

Overview
Canada’s Information Technology (IT) sector continues to expand as organizations modernize infrastructure, adopt cloud technologies, and respond to growing cybersecurity threats. Across industries, strong demand remains for professionals with foundational IT skills, particularly in network administration, technical support, and infrastructure management.

National Employment Trends

One of the most significant shifts in the Canadian IT landscape is the increasing reliance on Managed Service Providers (MSPs). Small and medium-sized businesses frequently outsource IT operations, including network management, security monitoring, and technical support. This trend has created steady demand for versatile IT professionals capable of troubleshooting systems, maintaining networks, and supporting end users.

At the same time, organizations are rapidly migrating from traditional on-premise infrastructure to cloud-based and hybrid environments. Platforms such as Microsoft Azure and Amazon Web Services (AWS) are now central to business operations. As a result, employers increasingly seek candidates with strong networking fundamentals, endpoint management experience, and familiarity with cloud-integrated systems.

Cybersecurity has emerged as one of the fastest-growing sectors within IT. Rising incidents of ransomware, phishing attacks, and data breaches have significantly increased the need for security-aware IT professionals.

Atlantic Canada and New Brunswick Outlook

Within Atlantic Canada, Nova Scotia — particularly Halifax — remains the largest technology hub. However, New Brunswick represents a growing and increasingly competitive technology market. The province has attracted national and global companies seeking lower operating costs while maintaining access to skilled professionals. Fredericton has gained recognition as a major cybersecurity center in Canada, supported by research institutions and cybersecurity-focused employers.

For IT professionals in New Brunswick, opportunities are particularly strong in private-sector IT support, managed services, infrastructure roles, and cybersecurity-adjacent positions.

Employer Skill Priorities

Across Canada, employers consistently emphasize the importance of:

  • Strong troubleshooting and technical support skills
  • Networking fundamentals and infrastructure management
  • Cloud-connected system environments
  • Security awareness and best practices
  • Clear communication with technical and non-technical users
