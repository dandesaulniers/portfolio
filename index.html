About Me

I am currently a Network Administration student at New Brunswick Community College, bringing over three decades of practical technology experience to my formal transition toward IT infrastructure, networking, and cybersecurity.

As the founder of Hands Down Designs, I provide hands-on IT support, network configuration, and malware remediation services for small businesses, nonprofit organizations, and residential clients, supporting over twenty recurring clients and numerous one-time projects. Through this work, I regularly troubleshoot hardware and software issues, configure segmented networks, implement backup solutions, and collaborate with vendors to maintain reliable and secure systems. I approach technical challenges methodically and prioritize clear communication to ensure users understand both the problem and the solution.

My interest in network administration developed naturally as I supported increasingly complex environments, including full network rebuilds, firewall configurations, and wireless infrastructure upgrades. In parallel, assisting clients affected by cyber incidents (including malware infections, compromised accounts, and system recoveries) highlighted the importance of proactive security measures, system hardening, and user education.

I am actively developing my technical expertise through my studies at NBCC and industry certifications, including CompTIA IT Fundamentals (ITF+) and CompTIA A+ (Core 1). I believe that strong technical fundamentals, continuous learning, and real-world problem-solving are essential to becoming an effective and adaptable IT professional.
